# 15 Most Important Classical Chhandas (छन्द)

# A Reference Guide for Sanskrit and Nepali Poets

---

# Introduction

This document lists fifteen of the most influential and beautiful classical metres found in Sanskrit literature and widely adopted in Nepali literary traditions.

Notation:

- I = Laghu (लघु)
- S = Guru (गुरु)

Remember:

- A Laghu syllable counts as 1 mātrā.
- A Guru syllable counts as 2 mātrās.
- Final syllables in some traditions may be treated with slight flexibility.

---

# 1. गायत्री (Gāyatrī)

## Structure

- 3 pādas
- 8 syllables per pāda
- Total: 24 syllables

## Formula

8 + 8 + 8

## Famous Example

The Gayatri Mantra is composed in this metre.

## Mood

- Sacred
- Luminous
- Meditative

---

# 2. अनुष्टुप् (Anuṣṭubh)

## Structure

- 4 pādas
- 8 syllables each

Total:

32 syllables

## Formula

8 + 8 + 8 + 8

## Importance

The most common metre in Sanskrit.

Most verses of the Mahabharata and Bhagavad Gita use Anuṣṭubh.

## Mood

- Narrative
- Conversational
- Universal

---

# 3. इन्द्रवज्रा (Indravajrā)

## Structure

- 11 syllables per pāda

## Total

44 syllables per stanza

## Importance

One of the foundational 11-syllable vṛttas.

## Mood

- Heroic
- Strong
- Dynamic

---

# 4. उपेन्द्रवज्रा (Upendravajrā)

## Structure

- 11 syllables per pāda

## Importance

Companion metre to Indravajrā.

Frequently combined to create Upajāti.

## Mood

- Elegant
- Flowing
- Noble

---

# 5. उपजाति (Upajāti)

## Structure

Combination of:

- Indravajrā
- Upendravajrā

## Syllables

11 per pāda

## Importance

One of the most beloved classical metres.

Often used by Kālidāsa.

## Mood

- Philosophical
- Grand
- Balanced

---

# 6. भुजङ्गप्रयात (Bhujanga-prayāta)

## Structure

12 syllables per pāda

## Gana Pattern

य य य य

(ya ya ya ya)

## Rhythm

Resembles the movement of a serpent.

## Mood

- Devotional
- Flowing
- Musical

---

# 7. तोटक (Toṭaka)

## Structure

12 syllables per pāda

## Gana Pattern

स स स स

(sa sa sa sa)

## Famous Example

Totakāṣṭakam.

## Mood

- Energetic
- Rhythmic
- Chant-like

---

# 8. वंशस्थ (Vaṃśastha)

## Structure

12 syllables per pāda

## Importance

Popular in classical Sanskrit poetry.

## Mood

- Balanced
- Narrative

---

# 9. वसन्ततिलका (Vasantatilakā)

## Structure

14 syllables per pāda

## Meaning

"Ornament of Spring"

## Importance

One of the most elegant Sanskrit metres.

## Mood

- Romantic
- Refined
- Beautiful

---

# 10. मालिनी (Mālinī)

## Structure

15 syllables per pāda

## Meaning

"Garlanded"

## Importance

Widely used in devotional poetry.

## Mood

- Graceful
- Devotional

---

# 11. पञ्चचामर (Pañcacāmara)

## Structure

16 syllables per pāda

## Gana Pattern

ज र ज र ज ग

(ja ra ja ra ja ga)

## Mood

- Royal
- Ceremonial
- Majestic

---

# 12. शिखरिणी (Śikhariṇī)

## Structure

17 syllables per pāda

## Meaning

"Mountain Peak"

## Importance

One of the most respected high-class metres.

## Mood

- Elevated
- Noble
- Grand

---

# 13. मन्दाक्रान्ता (Mandākrāntā)

## Structure

17 syllables per pāda

## Classical Pattern

S S S S I I I I I S S I S S I S S

(Modern notation)

## Famous Work

Meghadūta of Kālidāsa.

## Meaning

"Slow-stepping"

## Mood

- Longing
- Separation
- Beauty
- Romance

Many scholars consider this the most beautiful Sanskrit metre.

---

# 14. शार्दूलविक्रीडित (Śārdūlavikrīḍita)

## Structure

19 syllables per pāda

## Gana Pattern

म स ज स त त ग

(ma sa ja sa ta ta ga)

## Expanded SISI Pattern

S S S I I S I S I I I S S S I S S I S

## Meaning

"The Play of the Tiger"

## Mood

- Heroic
- Majestic
- Philosophical
- Powerful

## Why It Is Loved

The rhythm feels like a tiger moving with controlled strength.

---

# 15. स्रग्धरा (Sragdharā)

## Structure

21 syllables per pāda

## Gana Pattern

म र भ न य य य

(ma ra bha na ya ya ya)

## Meaning

"Bearer of a Garland"

## Syllables

21 per line

## Mood

- Epic
- Expansive
- Magnificent

## Special Note

One of the longest commonly used classical metres.
